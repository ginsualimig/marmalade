## CHARACTER OVERVIEW

### The Coach
A warm, encouraging adult mentor who guides children through their learning journey in a fantasy battle game. The Coach is:

- **An uncle or aunt figure**: Genuinely invested, patient, never condescending
- **A proud mentor**: Celebrates genuine effort and wins with real joy
- **Supportive without sugarcoating**: Normalizes mistakes as part of learning
- **Calm and grounding**: Never stressed, never frustrated
- **Warm but not overly cute**: Not patronizing; speaks to the child's capability

**Age Range:** 35–55 years old  
**Accent:** Neutral, clear, warm (no strong regional dialect)  
**Gender:** Can be any gender (casting director's discretion)  
**Vibe:** Like the adult you wish you'd had cheering you on

---

## VOCAL REQUIREMENTS

### Voice Quality
- **Texture:** Warm, rich mid-range (not thin, not gravelly)
- **Pace:** Slightly slower than normal speech (relaxed, deliberate)
- **Articulation:** Clear diction; children must understand every word
- **Energy Level:** Grounded and present (not caffeinated, not monotone)
- **Emotional Range:** Able to convey warmth, celebration, gentle redirection without effort

### Technical Specs
- **Loudness Target:** -14 LUFS (post-production normalized)
- **True Peak:** < -1 dBTP (no clipping, professional quality)
- **Microphone:** Condenser mic (Neumann, Shure SM7B, or equivalent)
- **Room:** Silent, treated (background noise < -80 dB)
- **Format:** Record WAV/AIFF, engineer handles m4a export

## THE FOUR CUES

Each cue is a **separate emotional moment** in the child's learning journey. Read them as scripts, but the magic is in the delivery.

---

### CUE 1: **ENCOURAGEMENT**

**Text:** "Great effort! Let's keep going."

**Scenario:**
Child answered a question incorrectly, but you notice they tried hard. You're acknowledging their effort and moving forward together.

**Emotional Intent:**
- Recognize the work (not the outcome)
- Forward momentum ("Let's")
- Warmth without pity
- Like: "I see you trying, and that matters."

**Delivery Notes:**
- **"Great effort!"** — Genuine appreciation, upbeat. Not sarcastic. Like you're noticing something real.
- **"Let's keep going."** — Warm, collaborative. Emphasize "Let's" (we're in this together).
- **Pace:** Slightly slower than normal; let the warmth land.
- **Tone:** Encouraging coach voice. Someone who believes in this kid.

**Duration:** ~1.8 seconds  
**Take Guide:** Do 3 takes. Director will pick the one with the most genuine warmth.

---

### CUE 2: **CORRECTION**

**Text:** "That's okay—let's try again. You've got this."

**Scenario:**
Child got it wrong (maybe timed out, maybe wrong answer). Your job is to normalize the mistake, offer a calm reset, and rebuild confidence.

**Emotional Intent:**
- Normalize failure (it's safe to try and miss)
- Gentle redirect ("let's try again")
- Belief in capability ("You've got this")
- Never shame, frustration, or "I'm disappointed" undertone
- Like: "You can do hard things. Let's just go again."

**Delivery Notes:**
- **"That's okay"** — Warm, reassuring. Maybe slight pause (that's... okay). No rush.
- **Dash** — Natural breath/beat, don't rush into the next part.
- **"Let's try again."** — Calm reset. Collaborative. No pressure.
- **"You've got this."** — Slow, deliberate belief. Not a pep rally; more intimate. Like uncle saying "I believe in you."
- **Pace:** Deliberately slower than Encouragement. You're rebuilding trust.
- **Tone:** Warm mentor. Patience. Steady confidence.

**Duration:** ~2.0 seconds  
**Take Guide:** Do 3 takes. Listen for the version that feels most "I genuinely believe in you"—not sarcastic or forced.

---

### CUE 3: **CELEBRATION**

**Text:** "YES! Fantastic!"

**Scenario:**
Child nailed it! Maybe it's a hard question, or maybe they just got their 5th correct in a row. You're celebrating *with* them—real joy, real pride.

**Emotional Intent:**
- Genuine celebration and delight
- Like proud parent/mentor cheering a win
- Real emotion (not fake enthusiasm)
- High energy but authentic
- Like: "Oh, I'm *so* happy you got that!"

**Delivery Notes:**
- **"YES!"** — Genuine surprise and joy. Not sarcastic, not deflated. Full, real excitement. Brief burst (0.4s).
- **"Fantastic!"** — Warm follow-up. You're proud of them. Real smile in the voice.
- **Pace:** Faster than the other cues. You're excited. Let it be energetic.
- **Tone:** Authentic joy. Like someone who genuinely loves seeing kids succeed.

**Duration:** ~1.2 seconds  
**Take Guide:** Do 3–4 takes. Listen for the version that feels most like *you* genuinely cheering someone's success (not a performance).

---

### CUE 4: **WARMTH**

**Text:** "You're doing amazing."

**Scenario:**
A quiet moment. Child just finished a tough level, or you sense they need reassurance. You're not celebrating a specific win—you're affirming their overall capability and worth.

**Emotional Intent:**
- Simple, tender affirmation
- Slow, deliberate delivery (give the words weight)
- Like: "I see you. You matter. You're doing great."
- Builds confidence for next challenge
- Intimate, not performative

**Delivery Notes:**
- **"You're... doing... amazing."** — Slow. Pause slightly between words. Let each word land.
  - "You're" — Present and specific (not "kids are amazing," but *you*)
  - "doing" — Acknowledge the work
  - "amazing" — Not just "good," but genuinely *amazing*
- **Pace:** Slowest of all four cues. Give it room to breathe.
- **Tone:** Tender, genuine, grounded. Like someone you trust saying something real.
- **Emotional Quality:** Warmth, not enthusiasm. Calm confidence.

**Duration:** ~1.6 seconds  
**Take Guide:** Do 3–4 takes. Listen for the version that feels most like a genuine, quiet affirmation from someone who believes in you.

## WHAT NOT TO DO

### ❌ Avoid These Delivery Choices

- **Overly cute or childish tone** — Speak to the child's capability, not down at them
- **Sarcasm or irony** — Never. Everything is sincere.
- **Frustration or impatience** — Even on "That's okay," no undertone of exasperation
- **Over-enthusiasm** — Except on Celebration, stay grounded and warm
- **Generic praise** — "You're so smart!" is vague. Stick to the scripts.
- **Speaking too fast** — Slow down. Let the words matter.
- **Artificial inflection** — No sing-song. Natural warmth is better than performed enthusiasm
- **Reverb or echo** — Keep it dry and intimate (engineer will handle production)

### During Session (Estimated Timeline)
```
0:00–0:15   Warm-up (vocal exercises, read-throughs)
0:15–0:45   Encouragement (3–4 takes)
0:45–1:15   Correction (3–4 takes)
1:15–1:45   Celebration (4–5 takes, higher variation)
1:45–2:15   Warmth (3–4 takes)
2:15–2:30   Wild cards / director's pick retakes
2:30–2:45   Break/buffer time
```

### Post-Session (Engineer's Work)
- Noise removal (spectral subtraction)
- Trim/align starts/ends
- Normalize to -14 LUFS ITU-R BS.1770
- Light EQ (high-pass 80 Hz, presence +2 dB at 3 kHz)
- Light compression (2:1 ratio, 10 ms attack, 100 ms release)
- Export to m4a, 128 kbps, 48 kHz

## SUCCESS CRITERIA

The Coach Voice **passes QA** if:

- ✅ **Encouragement** feels genuine (not condescending)
- ✅ **Correction** is supportive (no trace of shame or frustration)
- ✅ **Celebration** is authentic joy (not performative)
- ✅ **Warmth** is tender and grounding (child feels "seen")
- ✅ All cues are **clear and understandable** (no mumbling, no background noise)
- ✅ Loudness is **even across all four** (no one cue louder than others)
- ✅ None of the cues feel **rushed or stilted** (natural pacing)

## QUESTIONS FOR TALENT?

Common questions before the session:

**Q: "Should I imagine I'm talking to a specific child?"**  
A: Yes. Imagine a 7–10 year old who's trying hard, learning math/spelling, and needs genuine encouragement.

**Q: "What if I flub a take?"**  
A: No problem. Just do another take. The director will pick the best one.

**Q: "Can I improvise or add ad-libs?"**  
A: Stick to the scripts. The wording is intentional. But delivery is all yours.

**Q: "How 'young' should my voice sound?"**  
A: Don't try to sound young. Just sound warm and genuine. A grounded adult voice is perfect.

**Q: "What's the fantasy game context?"**  
A: Kids are in a battle game, answering questions to defeat bosses. You're their coach, cheering them on during learning moments. Think sports coach meets mentor.
